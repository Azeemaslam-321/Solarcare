Why customers choose an AMC

An annual maintenance contract creates predictability. Instead of waiting until a complaint appears or dirt becomes obvious, the site gets a planned service rhythm. That rhythm is often what makes the biggest difference on larger or dust-prone rooftops.

What should be included in a practical plan

  • A clearly defined visit schedule, such as monthly or quarterly
  • Cleaning suited to the actual site condition
  • Basic inverter and output observation during visits
  • A simple summary or report after service
  • Clear expectations for minor issue follow-up
Good AMC plans reduce uncertainty: the customer knows what is covered, and the service team works against a clear routine rather than only reacting to problems.

Useful for home and society sites

AMC planning helps when the rooftop needs repeat cleaning and occasional visual inspection support.

More than cleaning alone

A stronger plan combines cleaning with observation, reporting, and earlier issue spotting.

Why commercial sites benefit more

Commercial rooftops, institutions, and larger solar sites usually feel small performance drops more quickly. Structured maintenance turns the service model from reactive to planned, which improves oversight and reduces the chance that issues stay unnoticed for too long.

Questions worth asking before choosing a plan

  • How often will the team actually visit?
  • Does the plan include inspection support or only cleaning?
  • Will the customer receive a service summary?
  • How are minor faults handled if found during a routine visit?

Is an AMC useful for home rooftops too?

Yes, especially when the rooftop gets dusty quickly or the owner wants predictable service instead of irregular one-time visits.

Should an AMC include inspection support?

A stronger AMC usually does. Cleaning alone is helpful, but inspection support adds more long-term value.

Why do larger rooftops benefit more?

Because even small efficiency losses can scale quickly across bigger systems, making planned maintenance more important.
