Common mistakes in solar panel cleaning

Cleaning solar panels may look simple, but improper methods can lead to poor results or accidental damage. Many people try random household tools without understanding what is safe.

Using hard brushes

Hard or rough brushes can leave marks or scratches on the glass surface. Gentle tools and careful handling are usually safer.

Using harsh chemicals

Strong cleaning liquids may not be suitable for solar panels. It is better to use safe methods that do not leave unwanted residue or create avoidable risk.

Cleaning at the wrong time

Very hot panel surfaces and strong afternoon heat are not always ideal for cleaning. Conditions should be safe and practical for both cleaning quality and worker safety.

Ignoring safety

Rooftop work should always be handled carefully, especially when panels are installed at height or in difficult-access areas.

Not cleaning regularly

Waiting too long can cause heavier buildup and make cleaning more difficult. Periodic maintenance usually keeps the system in better condition.
